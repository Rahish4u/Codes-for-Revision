Repetitive incidence of short amplitude food price pressures deters fall in inflation towards 4% target: RBI bulletin piece

The article has been prepared by RBI Deputy Governor Michael Patra and other central bank officials. The RBI said the views published in the article are of the authors and not of the institution.

Even as inflation is easing, the repetitive incidence of short amplitude food price pressures deters a swifter fall in headline inflation towards the target of 4 per cent, an article published in the Reserve Bank of India’s (RBI) monthly bulletin said. The monetary policy needs to guide inflation to the 4 per cent target while ensuring that the momentum of growth is sustained, it said. As per an in-house model, the GDP growth is likely to remain robust at 7.4 per cent during FY25, the article said. This estimate is higher than the RBI’s projection of the real GDP growth for 2024-25 at 7 per cent.
